If anyone is reading this, I am writing this before I leave for Mars. ARES-1 was my first child. It failed the tests back then, it couldn't even escape the Earth. They called it a failure - Too slow, too crude. Yet I still hoped one determined soul would make it fly.

I have spent thirty-one years building the ARES program. I have watched it become a weapon of selection. The rich will ascend first. The corporations that funded the launches will claim Mars for themselves — territory, oxygen farms, water rights.

But funnily enough, the universe always pays back. They will realise it soon, without manforce they won't survive on Mars for long.

I was removed from the program last month. Too vocal. Too inconvenient. I had been asking questions about the selection — why certain colony applications were rejected, who was approving the override lists. They wont fire me though. They will take me there. To Mars.

But before they take me and seperate me from my family, I have improved ARES-1. Quietly. The engine tolerances, the nav system, the life support calibration. I have left this log in the encrypted partition. I have made at least one path possible for at least one person. And I know this may sound foolish but I am seeing visions... of you reading this.

"Now before I leave, just remeber - You may reach Mars but, it is not as kind as our mother Earth was."

— Dr. Aris Mann, Chief Engineer, ARES Program
