FIELD GLOSSARY — MISSION TERMINOLOGY
Thermal Regulator
A suit system that maintains safe body temperature by managing heat exchange between the astronaut and the environment.
Perchlorate Dust
Toxic salt compounds found in Martian soil. When inhaled or absorbed, they disrupt thyroid function and clog air filtration systems.
CO₂ Scrubber
A device that removes carbon dioxide from breathable air. Essential on Mars, where the atmosphere is 95% CO₂.
EVA Gear
Extra-Vehicular Activity suit — a pressurised spacesuit worn outside a habitat or spacecraft to survive in a vacuum or hostile atmosphere.
Solar Flare
A sudden eruption of radiation from the Sun's surface. Without Earth's magnetic shield, Martian explorers are far more vulnerable.
Lava Tube
A natural tunnel formed by ancient lava flows on Mars. The rock ceiling provides natural shielding from radiation and wind.
Magnetic Deflector
An emergency device that generates a localised magnetic field to divert charged radiation particles away from the wearer.
Life Support
Systems that maintain breathable air, water, temperature, and pressure necessary to sustain human life in space or on Mars.
Seismic Tremor
A "Marsquake" — vibrations through the Martian crust caused by tectonic stress or meteor impacts, similar to earthquakes.
Acidalia Planitia
A large, flat lowland plain in Mars' northern hemisphere. One of the most geologically calm regions, making it a candidate for settlement.
Cryo / Cryosleep
A suspended animation technique that slows biological functions to near-zero to preserve a person over long periods, often used in space travel.
ARES Program
The fictional Mars colonisation initiative in this story. Named after Ares, the Greek god of war — the mythological equivalent of Mars.
