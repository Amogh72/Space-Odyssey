# Space Odyssey
It is an interactive, narrative-driven space flight simulator designed to educate users on the complexities of interplanetary travel while delivering a poignant message about environmental stewardship. The project was conceived to bridge the gap between dry scientific data and engaging gameplay, allowing players to step into the shoes of Thomas Mann as he navigates the void between Earth and Mars.

# Educational Philosophy
The core design goal was to move beyond passive learning. Instead of simply reading about Hohmann Transfer Orbits, RCS thrusters, or Radiation Shielding, players must manage these systems under pressure. By integrating real-world constraints—such as fuel-to-mass ratios, oxygen depletion, and orbital windows—the game forces users to think like engineers.
Every decision has a direct mathematical consequence on the ship’s integrity and the player's survival.

# Narrative & Visual Design
To ensure engagement, I created a dystopian setting with characters and organisations. The mystery of Dr. Aris Mann provides the emotional pull that keeps the player moving through the levels. 

# The Mission Message
Ultimately, the project is a reflection on our current relationship with Earth. The final revelation in the game emphasizes that while we have the technology to reach the stars, Mars is a harsh and unforgiving host. The story's conclusion serves as a reminder: The red planet may not be as kind as our Mother Earth was. We must take care of the world we inhabit before we are forced to flee into the dark.

# Key Features:
Physics-Based Choices:** Realistic orbital mechanics and survival variables.
Dynamic HUD:** A custom interface that tracks ship vitals and mission progress in real-time.
Branching Narrative:** Multiple paths based on ship selection (Ghost Runner vs. Titan) and resource management.
